SMB Factor Regime

Long-only · Monthly rebalance · Two ETFs · Full market exposure
SMB ON Hold IWN — iShares Russell 2000 Value — captures market + value + size premiums
SMB OFF Hold IWD — iShares Russell 1000 Value — captures market + value premiums

Always fully invested. No leverage, no short. One rebalance per month at most.

Why this works

The size premium (SMB) is time-varying. It clusters: years of small-cap outperformance alternate with years of large-cap dominance. A logistic regression model trained on 100 years of Fama-French data can identify the regime well above random (AUC 0.665). Because both ETFs hold value stocks and track broad indexes, the strategy keeps full market beta at all times — the regime signal only decides how much size exposure to add on top.

Signal construction

1
Target: Will the next 12-month SMB return be positive?
Binary label built from French monthly data (1926–present).
2
Features: Rolling momentum, volatility, and drawdown of SMB, HML, and Mkt-RF over 1/3/6/12-month windows. 30 features total.
3
Model: Logistic Regression with L2 regularisation (C=0.1), walk-forward cross-validation (min-train 240m, embargo 6m, step 24m).
4
Live proxy: From 2000 onward, French SMB data has a ~2-month publication lag. IWM − SPY is used as a real-time SMB proxy (correlation 0.91 with French SMB). Lag cost: only 0.09%/yr — negligible.
5
Decision rule: If model probability > 0.52 → IWN. Otherwise → IWD. The model is in IWN ~35% of months and IWD ~65% of months.

Model probability & raw SMB proxy

HML (value premium) — always hold, no signal needed

The value premium (HML) was also studied for regime timing. The timing model achieved AUC 0.479 — essentially random. Always holding value stocks (IWD / IWN) beats any attempt to time HML entry/exit. The strategy therefore maintains permanent value exposure in both legs.
