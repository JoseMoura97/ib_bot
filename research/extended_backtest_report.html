Key Findings

Druckenmiller & Burry survive the longer window. Both stay above SPY at 10yr: Druckenmiller +8.7pp (23.9% vs 15.3%), Burry +8.1pp (23.4% vs 15.3%). Druckenmiller's alpha drops from 14.5% to 9.5% — still highly meaningful over 43 quarterly rebalances. Burry's alpha is almost unchanged (13.1% → 12.3%) — most consistent signal in the set.
Li Lu collapses over 10yr. 3yr CAGR was 24.5% (Sharpe 1.34, alpha 9.6%) — looked like an elite strategy. 10yr: 10.2% CAGR, Sharpe 0.54, alpha 1.1% — underperforms SPY by 5pp. The 3yr numbers were entirely a function of timing: Li Lu was heavily concentrated in BABA/Alibaba and Chinese tech that happened to have a strong final 2 years after the regulatory crackdown recovery. 185 total trades over 10yr = extremely few data points.
Tepper and Howard Marks barely clear SPY at 10yr. Tepper: +1.5pp alpha 3.2% (was +10.5pp alpha 9.6% at 3yr). Marks: +1.3pp alpha 3.6% (was +4.5pp alpha 8.1% at 3yr). MaxDD -50.7% is extreme for a "defensive" strategy — Oaktree is credit-heavy and the 2020 credit freeze was brutal. Not worth active management fees/complexity for 1-2pp edge.
FINRA Monthly: tracks SPY closely, different regime. 13.8% CAGR vs SPY 14.7% (7.5yr) — basically flat to benchmark on raw return. But beta 0.63 and alpha 5.5% are real: the strategy captures ~63% of market risk with ~5pp excess return, which means it significantly outperforms SPY on a risk-adjusted basis. MaxDD -33.8% ≈ SPY -33.7% over the same window (mainly the 2020 crash).

MaxDD Reality Check

The 3yr window missed the two biggest drawdowns of the period:

  • COVID crash (Feb–Mar 2020): SPY -34%. Credit-heavy portfolios (Marks) worse at -50%+
  • 2022 bear market: SPY -25%, growth/tech funds (Tiger, Coatue) -40%+. The 2023 start date captured the recovery not the drawdown.

The extended MaxDDs are more representative of what real capital would experience.
